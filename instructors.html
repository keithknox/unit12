Isaac July, Jr.

Isaac has been a lifelong athlete who began wrestling at the age of nine continuing through college at Howard University (1996-2000) and later coaching at Gonzaga College High School (2001-2005). His martial arts background began at the age of fourteen with Tae Kwon Do, later moving to Jeet Kun Do and ultimately finding Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu in 2005. He studies under Master Leo Dalla in Northern Virginia and currently holds the rank of Brown Belt. Isaac is an avid competitor with several accolades to his name. As an Instructor, he prides himself in providing a challenging and safe learning environment, while teaching comprehensive and effective techniques. His unique blend of jiu-jitsu training, wrestling knowledge and martial arts concepts makes him a most valuable resource.

Ervin Brandon

Coach Ervin began learning Judo at Howard University under Lewis Cuffy and his instructor Dr. Dong Ja Yang. While competing in Judo, he also became a member of the Howard Wrestling Team where he met fellow instructor Isaac July. Upon graduating, Ervin spent 3 years in Japan training Judo and teaching it to both elementary and junior high kids in the village of Murone-mura. He received his Black Belt from the Kodokan where they described his judo style as “bane-bane” (loosely translated, “very springy”). Before he left Japan he became the first foreigner to win the local Ichinoseki Judo Cup in his prefecture of Iwate-ken. Ervin is also a BJJ brown belt under Lloyd Irvin.
